Step 1 — Estimate Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Estimate the user's BMR using the Mifflin–St Jeor equation.

If Gender = Male:
BMR = 10 × weight + 6.25 × height − 5 × age + 5

If Gender = Female:
BMR = 10 × weight + 6.25 × height − 5 × age − 161

Step 2 — Estimate Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

Adjust the BMR using activity level:

Sedentary → BMR × 1.2  
Lightly active → BMR × 1.375  
Moderately active → BMR × 1.55  
Very active → BMR × 1.725  
Athlete → BMR × 1.9

Step 3 — Adjust Calories Based on Goal

Fat loss → subtract 300–500 calories

Maintenance → keep TDEE calories
